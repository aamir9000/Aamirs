# AIRA — v5 STANDARD + ELEMENT LIBRARY + TRICK-SHOT LIBRARY
*The reference for rebuilding all 41 Special Concepts. Read once; draw from it so no two reels feel alike.*

## ⭐ v5 STANDARD (current — supersedes v3/v4)
1. **ONE place per concept, TRAVELLED THROUGH.** The location stays one coherent place (one palette/season/light
   family) — but she **moves through it**, so every frame is a **fresh view / stretch / vantage of that same
   place**, with the light evolving naturally as she travels. *Never* biome-hopping; *never* a frozen single spot
   re-shot from four angles. Think "travelling through a place — the place is the same, the views and visuals change."
2. **ONE magical element per concept, used ONCE, in a single RANDOM frame.** Never an element in every frame. It
   lands in **any one frame** (not always the climax) so placement feels unpredictable, and the **element differs for
   every concept** (see ELEMENT LIBRARY) — no element repeats across the set, so a viewer never sees the same magic twice.
3. **Subject does NOT dominate the frame.** Leave deliberate **negative space on the sides, top and bottom** — the
   world is a co-star. Mostly **wide / environment-forward** framing; she often sits smaller and off-centre. Tight
   crops are the **rare** exception, not the default.
4. **She still reads as the HERO — smartly.** In every concept she must be **shown clearly as the hero in ONE frame**
   (well-lit, styling + expression read, a hero beat) — but **vary which frame** that is across concepts (sometimes
   F1, sometimes F2/F3/F4) so reels don't all "reveal her at the end." Even the hero frame keeps some breathing room.
5. **TRICK SHOT(S) — a theme-suitable amount per concept (you judge).** Use the number of inventive camera/reveal
   shots that genuinely fits the concept's theme/energy — often 1, sometimes 2, rarely more; never forced. Place
   each in **any random frame**, and **vary which tricks** are used across concepts so no technique repeats.
6. **ELEMENT and TRICK SHOT NEVER COLLIDE.** The element's frame must be **different** from every trick-shot frame —
   they live in separate frames across the concept.
7. Keep everything else locked: 3 matched-angle stills per frame (Subject `Aira [placeholder]` + styling, plain bg /
   Object, plain bg / Environment = the view), identity-safe **facial expression written in the video prompt**, ONE
   continuous evolving camera move per clip (no internal cuts), real-time motion, two audio blocks (MASTER + in-frame),
   warm amber-hazel eyes, 9:16, loops.

> **Per concept you assign:** one PLACE (travelled through) · one ELEMENT (once, one random frame) · a
> theme-suitable number of TRICK SHOTS (random frame(s), **never the element's frame**) · one HERO frame (rotated) —
> each unique across the 41 so nothing feels repeated.

## 🎬 TRICK-SHOT LIBRARY (one signature trick per concept — vary which one)
*Inventive reveal/perspective beats. Many of these also serve rules #3 & #4 — they show her smartly (small, via a
reflection, or as a hero reveal) while keeping the world present.*

**1 · Reflection reveals**
- **Puddle mirror cross-through** *(the flagship):* open on a still, clean puddle holding the reflected sky/world; she rides/skates/walks **straight through it**, the mirror shattering into ripples as she passes — then tilt up to the real her.
- **Still-water mirror:** a lake/pool perfectly mirrors her + the world; a single leaf or drop breaks the glass.
- **Window / glass reflection:** she appears first in a shop window, train window or glass door, then we find the real her.
- **Polished-surface catch:** her reflection slides across a car door, a chrome kettle, a marble floor, mirror-sunglasses, or a brass bell.
- **Wet-street mirror:** after rain, the neon/sky reflection on wet pavement; she steps into it.

**2 · Foreground reveal / wipes**
- **Foreground wipe:** a passing train carriage, a market awning, a billowing sheet or a passer-by crosses the lens and "wipes" to reveal her.
- **Foliage parting:** the camera pushes through foreground leaves/flowers that part to reveal her beyond.
- **Rack-focus pull:** a sharp foreground object (lantern, bloom, hand, prayer-flag) goes soft as focus pulls to her behind it.
- **Bokeh-curtain:** defocused light orbs / fireflies fill the frame, then resolve to reveal her in the scene.
- **Fabric billow:** a silk scarf / curtain / flag billows across the lens and drops to reveal her.

**3 · Frame-within-frame**
- Through an **arch / doorway / gate / window / tunnel mouth**.
- **Between leaves / a flower-gap / a circle of hands / a ring of dancers**.
- Through the **spokes of a wheel**, a **parasol**, or a **gap in the crowd**.

**4 · Perspective / POV**
- **Subject-POV:** the world rushing past from her eyes — handlebar-cam / board-cam / boat-prow-cam / saddle-cam.
- **Trailing-object cam:** mounted to a trailing scarf, ribbon, balloon-string or kite-tail behind her.
- **Ground-level gap:** very low, the world framed under a bridge / branch / arch.
- **God's-eye top-down:** straight down as she moves through a pattern — lily pads, a spiral path, a market grid, snow tracks.

**5 · Crane / level reveals**
- **Crane-over-obstacle:** the camera rises over a hedge / wall / dune / wave to suddenly reveal the vista + her (she small).
- **Tunnel-rush:** through a tree-tunnel / colonnade / wave-curl / canyon, with her at the far bright end.
- **Reverse pull-out:** start tight on a tiny detail, pull way back to reveal she's small in a vast world.
- **Split-level:** half above / half below the water surface in one frame.

**6 · Match-cuts & motion links (across the cut)**
- **Shape match:** a spinning wheel → a spinning sun/parasol/fan; a round puddle → the round moon.
- **Motion handoff:** she releases something (a petal, scarf, ball) and the camera follows it into the next view.
- **Silhouette reveal:** she's a black silhouette against a bright sky/sunset, then light resolves her into the hero.
- **Shadow-first:** her long cast shadow slides into frame before she does.

## 👑 HERO-PRESENCE RULE (how she stays the star without repetition)
- Each concept has **exactly one clear HERO frame** — closer, well-lit, styling + expression + identity read strongly.
- **Rotate which frame is the hero** across the 41 (F1 / F2 / F3 / F4) — and rotate **how** she's revealed (hero
  reflection, silhouette-into-light, foliage-parting reveal, walk-toward-lens, turn-to-camera) so no two reels
  introduce her the same way. Every other frame keeps her smaller with the world dominant (rule #3).

## 🚫 NO-REPEAT ASSIGNMENT PRINCIPLE
Across all 41: no PLACE-type, no ELEMENT, no TRICK-SHOT, and no HERO-frame-position+reveal combination repeats as a
full set. Reuse of a broad category is fine only when its partners differ enough that the *beat* never reads twice.
