# IDEA B — THE WORLD REACTS TO HER MOOD
*Feeling: relatable. No mirror, no product — she's just living. The environment is her emotional weather made visible.*

## CONCEPT B-1 — "Monday Morning"

- **Feeling:** relatable frustration → satisfaction
- **Hairstyle (this concept):** messy high bun with loose strands falling, a sage-green velvet scrunchie barely holding, undone chic
- **Magic move:** the whole room physically **dims and sulks** when she's annoyed (flowers droop, colors drain) — then **blooms saturated** when she finds her groove
- **Concept line:** "even the room knows"
- **Continuous shot:** one unbroken move through a maximalist apartment on a grey morning, 7 frames
- **Loop:** final frame returns the room to its moody grey opening state, ready to cycle

### ☆ EXTERNAL AUDIO BLOCK — B-1 (background music / sound bed)
- **Genre / vibe:** cinematic lo-fi with a dramatic emotional arc — starts grey and minimal, blooms into rich warmth.
- **Tempo / key:** ~80 BPM, starts in A minor (grey), modulates to A major (bloom) at the turn.
- **Core elements:** muted piano on the grey frames; a soft vinyl crackle; building warm strings and a felt-kick as the bloom happens; a satisfying bass drop at the hero frame; strips back to muted piano on the loop.
- **Arc:** minimal grey piano (Frames 1–3) → a swelling transition riser (Frame 4 turn) → full warm strings + bass + bright piano (Frames 5–6) → decay back to minimal grey piano (Frame 7 loop close).
- **Texture note:** the transition from muted to full should feel like a cloud clearing — not abrupt, but deeply satisfying.
- **Loop:** clean 15s loop matching the visual loop point.
