## CONCEPT B-3 — "The Music Changed"

- **Feeling:** playful / mood shift relatable
- **Hairstyle (this concept):** two low pigtail braids, glossy, tied with thin black silk ribbons, ends brushing her collarbone
- **Magic move:** when she changes the song on her phone, the **entire environment morphs** to match the genre — jazz turns the room moody and smoky, pop makes it bright and candy-colored, classical makes it regal and grand — all around her while she cooks
- **Concept line:** "the music changed everything"
- **Continuous shot:** one unbroken move through a kitchen that shape-shifts, 7 frames
- **Loop:** final song-skip returns the kitchen to the exact opening state

### ☆ EXTERNAL AUDIO BLOCK — B-3 (background music / sound bed)
- **Genre / vibe:** this concept IS about music — so the external audio block is a **medley that shifts genre with the kitchen morph**. It's the actual music she's "playing."
- **Tempo / key:** shifts — jazz section (~100 BPM, Bb minor, swing) → pop section (~120 BPM, C major, four-on-floor) → classical section (~70 BPM, D major, orchestral) → back to neutral ambient on the loop.
- **Core elements per section:**
  - Jazz: upright bass, brushed snares, muted trumpet solo, warm Rhodes.
  - Pop: bright synth chords, clap-snap beat, a catchy vocal-chop hook, bubbly bass.
  - Classical: full strings, harp arpeggios, french horn, a grand piano.
- **Arc:** ambient kitchen sounds on Frame 1 → jazz fills the room (Frame 2) → a brief silence on the skip → pop drops (Frames 3–4) → brief silence → classical swells (Frame 5) → the laugh breaks it (Frame 6) → returns to ambient kitchen on the loop close (Frame 7).
- **Texture note:** each genre section should sound complete and authentic, not parody — the joke is in the environment morphing, not in the music being ironic.
- **Loop:** clean 14s loop matching the visual loop point.
