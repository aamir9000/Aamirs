# AIRA — 5 SPOKEN-CONTENT SCRIPTS (She Talks · Heavy-Detail)

<aside>
Five talking reels where Aira SPEAKS to camera. The craft here lives in the WRITING and DELIVERY, not in frame-physics — so there's no clip-chain or handoff. Each script gives: format, length, the visual setup, a scroll-stopping HOOK (first line), the full spoken script broken into timed beats with delivery direction (tone + expression + gesture), the on-screen action / B-roll, on-screen text, the CTA, the caption, and the audio plan. Voice: English, natural Indian accent, warm and real — she sounds like a friend, never an ad-reader. Aira stays adult, modest, identity-locked.
</aside>

## Global Spec
- Identity: AIRA reference-locked, adult, modest, non-sexualized. Lip-sync must match the script exactly; natural mouth shapes, no garble.
- Delivery law: conversational, warm, varied pace — she pauses, she laughs, she emphasizes; never monotone, never reading.
- Expression law: heavy facial expression direction per beat (brows, eyes, smile, micro-reactions) so the performance feels alive and fluid.
- Caption-friendly: every script works with burned-in subtitles (most people watch muted).
- Distinctness: the 5 scripts span different formats and feelings — GRWM, beauty/value, storytime, hot-take, day-in-life voiceover.
- Each beat format: [timecode] DELIVERY (tone/expression/gesture) — "spoken line." → VISUAL/B-ROLL · ON-SCREEN TEXT.

# SCRIPT 1 — GRWM: "Get Ready With Me for a First Date" (~38s, talking GRWM)
FORMAT: classic GRWM, she does makeup/hair while talking through the nerves of a first date. Feeling: relatable, warm, a little giddy.
VISUAL SETUP: soft warm vanity light, she's at a mirror mid-routine in a cosy robe (modest), products around; intimate eye-contact through the lens.
HOOK (0–3s): big-eyed, half-excited-half-nervous, leaning into the lens, brush in hand — "Okay so I have a first date in one hour and I am... not okay. Get ready with me." → quick close-up of her nervous-excited face, then a glance away and back. ON-SCREEN: "GRWM: first date edition 😭"
BEATS:
- [3–9s] LIGHT, laughing at herself, dabbing foundation — "I changed my outfit four times. FOUR. The floor of my room is a crime scene right now." → B-roll flash of a messy outfit pile. ON-SCREEN: "send help."
- [9–16s] SOFTER, focused on blending, a small sincere smile — "He's actually really sweet though. We've been texting for like two weeks and he remembered I hate coriander, so... the bar is on the floor but I'm obsessed." → she pauses, smiles down, then back to mirror.
- [16–24s] BUILDING, swiping on blush, more animated, eyebrows up — "Here's my thing — I'm not doing the most. A little glow, my favourite blush, the lip I feel like myself in. If he likes me, he likes ME, not a full beat I can't recreate." → close-up of the blush going on, natural flush. ON-SCREEN: "be the most YOU version."
- [24–32s] GENUINE, slowing down, looking right into lens, hand pausing — "Honestly? I'm nervous because I actually like him. And that's kind of nice. It's been a while since I cared enough to be nervous." → a soft vulnerable beat, real eyes.
- [32–38s] BRIGHT, snapping back, fluffing hair, a confident grin — "Okay. Lip on, perfume, deep breath. How do I look?" → she turns fully to camera, finished look, a little spin. ON-SCREEN: "wish me luck 🤞"
CTA: "Tell me your first-date nerves in the comments so I don't feel alone 😭 — and follow for how it went (I WILL be reporting back)."
CAPTION: "First date in an hour and my heart is in my throat 🫠 Doing a soft, very-me glow because the right person likes the real you. Wish me luck and manifest no awkward silences pls. 🤞 #grwm #firstdate #softglam"
AUDIO: her voice front and centre; a soft warm lo-fi beat low under it; natural product/brush sounds (ASMR-light). Subtitles burned in.

# SCRIPT 2 — VALUE/BEAUTY: "My honest 5-minute everyday glow" (~32s)
FORMAT: fast, useful, value-first beauty reel — she teaches her real quick routine while talking. Feeling: helpful best-friend, no-BS.
VISUAL SETUP: bright clean daylight bathroom/vanity, minimal products lined up, fresh modest top; energetic but warm.
HOOK (0–3s): direct to lens, finger up, confident — "If you have five minutes and you want to look alive — not 'full glam,' just alive — save this." → quick jump-cut of the 5 products in a row. ON-SCREEN: "5-MIN EVERYDAY GLOW ✨"
BEATS:
- [3–9s] BRISK, applying, looking between mirror and lens — "Step one, skin tint not foundation. We want skin that looks like skin. Three dots, fingers, blend out. Done in twenty seconds." → close-up of the sheer application. ON-SCREEN: "1. skin tint (not foundation)".
- [9–15s] WARM, smiling, tapping cheeks — "Cream blush HIGH on the cheeks. This is the whole trick. It's what makes you look like you slept eight hours and drink water." → blush tap, natural flush blooms. ON-SCREEN: "2. cream blush = fake good sleep".
- [15–21s] PLAYFUL, brushing brows up — "Brows brushed straight up. That's it. No drawing. Instant 'I have my life together' energy." → brow-brush close-up. ON-SCREEN: "3. brows UP".
- [21–27s] CONFIDENT, dabbing lid — "One cream shade on the lid with your finger, blend the edges, and a little on the inner corner so you look awake." → eye close-up, soft glow. ON-SCREEN: "4. one-finger eye".
- [27–32s] FINISHING, glossy smile to lens — "Lip oil, press your lips, and go live your life. Five minutes. You're done." → final look, a little head-tilt. ON-SCREEN: "5. lip oil → GO. ⏱️".
CTA: "Save this for tomorrow morning and follow — I'm doing a 'going out' version next. What look do you want? 👇"
CAPTION: "The 5-minute routine I actually do when I'm running late. No full coverage, no 12 steps — just looking alive and getting out the door. Save it!! ✨ #beauty #makeuproutine #5minutemakeup #glowup"
AUDIO: upbeat clean track, her voice on top, satisfying product taps; quick punchy cuts on each step. Subtitles burned in.

# SCRIPT 3 — STORYTIME: "The DM I should NOT have replied to" (~40s)
FORMAT: storytime — she tells a funny/relatable personal story to camera, building to a punchline. Feeling: juicy, funny, "tell me everything" energy.
VISUAL SETUP: casual, sitting on her bed/couch in cosy clothes (modest), leaning into the camera like she's gossiping with you; warm lamp light.
HOOK (0–4s): wide eyes, a disbelieving little laugh, leaning in close — "So a guy I went on ONE date with two years ago just slid back into my DMs and you are not going to believe what he said. Get comfy." → she pulls a pillow into her lap. ON-SCREEN: "storytime: the audacity 💀"
BEATS:
- [4–12s] SETTING UP, animated hands — "Okay context: ONE date. Two years ago. He talked about his ex for forty-five minutes and then asked ME to split the dessert he ordered. I never texted back. Iconic of me." → she nods, proud. ON-SCREEN: "ONE date. 45 min about his ex. 🚩"
- [12–22s] BUILDING, mock-gasp, hand to chest — "So yesterday — two years of silence — my phone buzzes. And it's him. And the message says..." → dramatic pause, she bites her lip, eyebrows up — "...'Hey stranger, you've been on my mind.' STRANGER. Sir, you made me split a brownie." → she throws her head back laughing. ON-SCREEN: "'hey stranger' 🙃".
- [22–31s] WRY, lower voice, conspiratorial — "And the OLD me would've spiralled, screenshotted it to seven group chats, written a paragraph. But new me?" → a slow confident smile. — "I looked at it. I felt nothing. And I put my phone face-down and finished my chai." → calm sip-mime.
- [31–40s] WARM, sincere turn, soft smile to lens — "And that's the whole story. Not because I'm cold — because I finally like my own peace more than I like the drama. Growth is boring and it feels amazing." → genuine, settled. ON-SCREEN: "peace > drama. we grew. 🌱".
CTA: "Tell me your 'hey stranger' story, I KNOW you have one 😭 Follow for part two if he double-texts (he will)."
CAPTION: "'Hey stranger.' Babe we split a brownie, we are not strangers, we are barely acquaintances 💀 Anyway — the real glow-up is feeling nothing. Drop your DM horror stories below 👇 #storytime #datingstories #growth #relatable"
AUDIO: just her voice (storytime = intimacy), maybe the faintest bed of room-tone; no loud music so the words land. Subtitles burned in, emphasised words highlighted.

# SCRIPT 4 — HOT TAKE: "Why I stopped chasing the perfect morning routine" (~35s)
FORMAT: opinion/hot-take to camera — a mini-monologue with a point of view. Feeling: refreshing, a little contrarian, validating.
VISUAL SETUP: she's mid-ordinary-morning (real, slightly messy kitchen), holding a mug, talking casually — NOT a polished aesthetic; that's the point. Soft daylight.
HOOK (0–4s): flat, honest, a knowing look to lens, mid-sip — "Unpopular opinion: the '5am, ice bath, journal, 10-step morning' is making you feel worse, not better. Let me explain." → she shrugs, sits on the counter. ON-SCREEN: "hot take ☕".
BEATS:
- [4–12s] BUILDING, gesturing with the mug — "I tried it. The whole thing. And every day I 'failed' the routine by 6:15 I felt like garbage before my day even started. We turned rest into another job." → eye-roll, half-laugh. ON-SCREEN: "we made mornings a PERFORMANCE."
- [12–22s] SINCERE, leaning in — "So here's what I actually do now: I wake up when my body's done sleeping. I drink my coffee before I touch my phone. And I do ONE nice thing — open a window, stretch, whatever. That's it. That's the routine." → calm, certain. ON-SCREEN: "wake up → coffee → ONE nice thing.".
- [22–30s] WARM, softer — "And weirdly? I'm calmer than I've ever been. Because it's not about doing twelve things at dawn. It's about not starting your day already at war with yourself." → a real, settled smile.
- [30–35s] DIRECT, a gentle challenge to lens — "Your morning doesn't need to be optimised. It needs to be kind. Try it tomorrow." → confident nod, sip. ON-SCREEN: "be kind to your mornings 🤍".
CTA: "Tell me — are you team 5am-grind or team wake-up-naturally? I need to know I'm not alone 👇 Follow for more 'permission to chill' content."
CAPTION: "Nobody's morning looks like the videos and that's okay. I stopped optimising my mornings and started actually enjoying them. Coffee before phone changed my LIFE. ☕🤍 Agree or fight me in the comments 👇 #hottake #morningroutine #slowliving #mentalhealth"
AUDIO: her voice lead, a calm warm track very low; intentionally un-flashy edit (few cuts) so it feels honest. Subtitles burned in.

# SCRIPT 5 — DAY-IN-LIFE VOICEOVER: "A slow Sunday" (~33s, voiceover over B-roll)
FORMAT: soft voiceover narration over aesthetic day-in-life B-roll — she's not on-camera talking the whole time; her voice narrates while we see her Sunday. Feeling: cosy, reflective, romanticise-your-life. (She appears in the B-roll; the talking is VO.)
VISUAL SETUP: a series of warm, slow, beautiful Sunday B-roll clips; her voice is calm, low, intimate — like reading a diary entry.
HOOK (0–4s) [VO over a slow shot of morning light hitting the bed, her stirring awake]: warm, unhurried — "Nobody tells you that the most healing thing in the world is a Sunday with absolutely nowhere to be." → ON-SCREEN: "a slow Sunday 🤍".
BEATS:
- [4–11s] [VO over: pouring coffee, steam, bare feet on a wooden floor, opening a window] gentle — "I made my coffee slow. I let the morning be quiet. I didn't reach for my phone first thing, and the world kept turning anyway." → soft natural sounds under.
- [11–19s] [VO over: her reading by a window, a plant being watered, a candle lit, soft rain or sun] reflective — "I think we forget that rest isn't lazy. Doing nothing, on purpose, is how you hear yourself again." → she turns a page, a small content smile.
- [19–27s] [VO over: cooking something simple, a face-mask moment, laughing at something on the counter] warmer — "I cooked something just for me. I took care of myself the way I'd take care of someone I love. No rushing. No guilt." → genuine candid joy.
- [27–33s] [VO over: golden-hour light, her wrapped in a blanket on the couch, eyes closing softly, a calm exhale] tender, almost a whisper — "And by evening I felt like a person again. Protect your slow Sundays. They're putting you back together." → fade on her peaceful face. ON-SCREEN: "protect your peace. 🌙".
CTA: "This is your sign to do nothing this Sunday. Save it, send it to someone who needs rest, and follow for more soft-life reminders. 🤍"
CAPTION: "A slow Sunday is self-care nobody can sell you. Coffee before phone, doing nothing on purpose, taking care of myself like I matter. Because I do. And so do you. 🌙 Save this for your next reset day. #slowliving #selfcare #dayinmylife #softlife #sundayreset"
AUDIO: her soft VO is the hero; a tender ambient piano/lo-fi bed very low; rich diegetic sounds (coffee pour, rain, page turn) for ASMR warmth. Subtitles burned in, gentle.
