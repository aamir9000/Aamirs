## [THE FIVE-PILLAR GRWM RETENTION ARCHITECTURE — MANDATORY]

Every concept must be engineered with FIVE retention mechanics running simultaneously across the frame sequence. GRWM has one extra mechanic over the parent LUMINA protocol because ritual content lives or dies on transformation legibility.

HOOK MECHANIC: Hooks are distributed across multiple frames, not concentrated on Frame 1. Every concept contains at least 3 distinct hook beats placed at strategic frames — bare-faced direct eye-contact lock, towel-pulled-back wet-hair reveal, mirror double-take with raised brow, dropper held above cheek macro, lip-product unscrew macro, hair-clip release with gravity drop, robe-tie loosened beat, perfume atomizer pressed at neck, finished-look mirror wink. Frame 1 may use a bare-faced confrontation hook OR a towel-wrapped post-shower entrance OR a robe-tied vanity-sit hook — but never a calm sitting-still pose without engagement. The viewer must feel arrested within two seconds.

ESCALATION MECHANIC: Energy never plateaus. Each frame either deepens the ritual intimacy, escalates a transformation beat, introduces a new product, changes the camera language, or shifts the lighting moment as time-of-day progresses through the ritual. The concept arc moves from bare-faced beginning to mid-ritual focused application to finished-look sovereign reveal — like a short ritual film's three-act compression.

CONTACT MECHANIC: Every frame must contain at least one moment of human-product or human-environment physical contact — fingertip in cream, dropper above cheek, lipstick bullet to lower lip, mascara wand to lash root, hairbrush through wet hair, atomizer at clavicle, towel against jaw, robe sash through fingers, mirror-fog wiped with palm. No frame may exist where the subject is merely present in space without making deliberate readable contact with a beauty product or grooming surface.

PAYOFF MECHANIC: The final frame of every concept must deliver a finished-look cinematic seal — sovereign mirror wink at finished face, flying kiss to lens at finished face, settled smile direct to lens at finished face, or the over-shoulder warm farewell as she rises from the vanity. The transformation must read clearly between Frame 1 (bare-faced or undone) and Frame Final (finished). The ending must feel earned like the last shot of a luxury ritual film.

TRANSFORMATION LEGIBILITY MECHANIC: This is the GRWM-specific pillar. Across the reel the subject must visibly progress through readable beauty stages — bare skin → skincare → base → eyes → lips → hair styled → fragrance → finished. Every concept maps these stages explicitly in the Concept Header. The viewer at Frame Final must see a different woman from the woman at Frame 1, achieved entirely through real product application — never through cuts that hide the work.

## [SPATIAL ANCHOR LOCK — GRWM MIRROR CLAUSE]

Image generators sometimes mirror-flip output when handedness is ambiguous. The fix is mandatory frame-space anchoring on every Image Prompt. GRWM concepts add one extra rule because mirrors are everywhere in this protocol.

RULE 1 — Every Image Prompt Layer 2 begins with:
SPATIAL ANCHOR: subject positioned in left-third/center-third/right-third of frame, body angled toward camera-left/camera-right/camera, named shoulder closer to camera, dominant light direction, key environment object positions. Frame is NOT mirrored.

RULE 2 — Use camera-relative coordinates only: camera-left, camera-right, frame-left, frame-right, foreground, background.

RULE 3 — Every light source includes camera-side position and angle.

RULE 4 — Every key environment object receives fixed frame-position anchoring.

RULE 5 — Every held product is double-anchored to both hand and frame-position.

RULE 6 — Environment orientation remains continuity-locked across frames unless deliberate camera rotation is explicitly stated.

RULE 7 — Anatomy lock must support the Spatial Anchor orientation.

RULE 8 — GRWM MIRROR CLAUSE: When the frame includes a mirror, Layer 2 must explicitly state whether the camera is capturing the subject directly, the subject's reflection only, or both subject and reflection in frame. The reflected image is not considered a mirror-flip of the composition — the composition is still NOT mirrored. The Spatial Anchor describes the real subject's position, not the reflected position. If the camera is reading the reflection only, Layer 2 must state: camera frames the mirror reflection only; the subject's real body is at named off-frame position.

RULE 9 — Layer 2 closes with one of:
- Composition is locked. Frame is NOT mirrored.
- Spatial layout is fixed. Frame is NOT mirrored.
- Camera-side orientation is locked. Frame is NOT mirrored.

The Spatial Anchor Lock integrates directly into Layer 2 of every Image Prompt.
